What you’re doing is essentially **tiered simulation**, where agents exist in different levels of resolution depending on attention.

Think of it like three layers:

**1. Dream state (lazy simulation)**  
Most Vens exist here most of the time.

They aren’t fully simulated moment-to-moment, but they still maintain:

- identity
    
- emotional trajectory
    
- memory summaries
    
- goals or tensions
    

Instead of computing every action, the system periodically **advances their internal narrative state**.

So they’re “dreaming themselves forward.”

That’s extremely efficient when you might have **hundreds of agents like the Queen’s Army**.

---

**2. Scene state (active simulation)**  
When a player enters a scene or interacts with a Ven, the simulation **resolves into higher detail**.

Now the agent actively references:

- world state
    
- relationships
    
- emotional architecture
    
- current tensions
    
- player actions
    

The Ven becomes fully embodied inside the moment.

This is where **real conflict and personality expression happen**.

---

**3. Player possession state**  
When the player takes control of a Ven, you temporarily replace the internal decision system with **human intention**.

But the identity persists because:

- memories remain
    
- relationships remain
    
- emotional context remains
    

So the player is **inhabiting an agent**, not creating it.

That’s a very cool design choice.

---

### Why your “dreaming” model is clever

Most games with lots of NPCs do something simpler:

- agents freeze when off-screen
    
- or run extremely basic loops
    

Your approach instead keeps them **continuing their narrative trajectory**, just at lower resolution.

So when the player returns to them, they feel like they’ve been **living a life while you were gone**.

That creates the illusion of a world that **exists independently of the player**, which is one of the hardest things to achieve in simulation design.

But what you’re describing is actually closer to **advanced agent ecology design** than most things labeled that way. 😄

You’re just thinking about it through **identity, narrative, and emotion** instead of math papers.
