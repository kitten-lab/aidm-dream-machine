# ADM — VOX Packs

VOX packs define how narrative is expressed to the player.

They are creator-authored systems that transform structured world state into a specific narrative voice, tone, and style.

---

## Separation of Logic and Expression

ADM’s reasoning layer operates on structured, factual data:

- what entities (Vens) know  
- what they have experienced  
- current relationships and states  
- recent events and context  

This layer does not produce narrative prose.

Instead, it produces a resolved narrative state based on logic and evaluation.

---

## Role of VOX Packs

VOX packs take this resolved state and render it into narrative.

They:
- define tone, voice, and style  
- shape how events are described  
- determine how characters and environments are presented  
- filter and adapt output through the player’s perspective  

The same underlying event can be expressed differently depending on the active VOX pack.

---

## Creation

VOX packs are created within the ADM Studio.

Creators (e.g. writers, novelists) work with AI-assisted tools to:
- define narrative style  
- generate example outputs  
- refine voice and structure  

These packs can then be used across worlds.

---

## Player Application

Each player experiences the world through an active VOX pack.

- The VOX pack is applied to the player’s Ven (their active identity)
- Narrative output is filtered through this lens
- Different players may experience the same world differently based on their VOX pack

---

VOX packs allow ADM to separate:
- **what happens** (logic and state)  
from  
- **how it is experienced** (narrative expression)
