# ADM — Overview

ADM is a system for generating and running interactive, roleplay-driven worlds.  
It creates environments where narrative emerges through player interaction, system simulation, and AI-driven entities.

The core goal of ADM is to produce satisfying narrative experiences, and to continuously generate new narrative from those interactions.

In ADM, satisfaction comes from convergence — when characters, events, and narrative threads come together in a way that feels coherent and meaningful.  
Unlike traditional stories, where convergence often leads to an ending, ADM is designed to continue beyond resolution.

Narrative does not conclude and disappear.  
Instead, resolved threads become the foundation for new developments, allowing players to remain within the world and continue building meaning over time.

ADM is not tied to a single setting or genre.  
It can express different world types and styles while maintaining the same underlying system for narrative generation and interaction.
